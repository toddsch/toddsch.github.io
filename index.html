Thanks for checking out my portfolio.

Growing up, I always felt at peace around technology. It's a comfortable thing to know that, in the chaos of life, you can rely on something to generate the same outcome every time, no matter what.

Well... that's what I thought until I started writing software.

Since shattering my entire view of the world, I've put my all into developing applications in several areas of focus:

  • GPS & iBeacon
  • Cryptocurrency
  • Social Media
  • Food Recipe Handling
  • Music Player
  • Chat bot
  • Language learning
  • many more, soon...

I've been writing software since 2011. Since graduating from university, I've worked with a startup dealing with Bitcoin in Kamloops, British Columbia and wrote software to compare prices of cryptocurrency across several exchanges.

After that I found myself living overseas in Shanghai, China (wow!) where I spearheaded the design and development of a new social app, Magnet. Using GPS, iBeacon tech and general surroundings, it connected nearby people to a central forum for that location — typically tied to a venue.

During my time at Magnet I also worked for their sister company OpenLanguage where I deployed standard bugfixes for their flagship app. I also designed and developed a "Lite" version of their app (named Kaiyan) meant only for playing and maintaining a personal library of podcasts from OpenLanguage.

I've since returned to Canada and picked up contract work where available: for Bitnational, a company in Vancouver that specializes in operating a network of bitcoin ATMs. When not working professionally I'll chip away at ongoing personal projects, like a chatbot to use with my friends or a music player app; really, anything that comes to mind and sounds interesting or challenging.

Finally, I have no experience with it yet, but I keep noticing AI staring me down from the horizon. Soon.
